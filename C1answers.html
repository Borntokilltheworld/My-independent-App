II.Answer the following

1. According to Minhaj -i -siraj, a chronicler . Hindustan was the land between the river Ganga and Yamuna. Babur used the term Hindustan to describe the geography , the fauna and the culture of the inhabitants of the sub- continent.

2.Coins, Inscriptions, architecture , textual records are some of the sources of History.

3. During the medieval period , paper became cheaper and came into common use . Paper was widely available. Holy texts, chronicles, letters, teachings and petitions were written on paper. Manuscripts were collected by rich people, rulers, monasteries and even temples. They were stored in libraries and archives. Most of these were copied by hand. Paper has become an integral part of our lives and also for Historians.

4.Scribe is a court official who can translate and write on behalf of others. Scribes copies manuscripts and also makes small changes.

5. The period between 700 -1750 saw many changes. New technologies were introduced. Persian wheels in irrigation ,spinning wheel in weaving and fire arms in combat were introduced. New food and beverages like potato, corn, chilies, tea and coffee were introduced. People also travelled long distance in search of opportunity and wealth. Rajputs gained more importance . Kshatriyas and their contribution to rulers became important. Independent kingdom like marathas , Sikhs, jats, Ahoms became more prominent. Agriculture became important. Many complex societies developed. Jats were given more importance.

6. Jatis became more prominent during the medieval period. People were grouped into jatis on basis of their back ground and the occupation they do. The status of Jati varied from area to area. Jatis framed their own rules and regulations to manage the conduct of their members The regulations were enforced by an assembly of elders known as Jati Panchayat. The villagers were governed by chieftains (group leaders).

7. People belief in the divine became personal. Collective belief in super natural agency and religion was connected with social and economic organization of local communities. In Hinduism , worship of new deities, constructions of temples by royalty and growing importance of Brahmans became prominent. Learning of Sanskrit gained importance. Knowledge in Sanskrit was highly respected. Bhakthi movements became more popular during the medieval period. New religious like Islam and Christianity spread up.
